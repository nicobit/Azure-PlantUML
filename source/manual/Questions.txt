1️⃣ Where is the legal shareholder register?

This is the most important question.

Where is the official shareholder registry legally maintained?

Possible answers:

Answer	Architecture
Transfer Agent system	Mirror OSS
Digital TA platform	Hybrid
Smart contract	Fully on-chain

If the registry is off-chain → blockchain must mirror the registry.

2️⃣ Who acts as the Transfer Agent?

Ask:

Who is the registered Transfer Agent responsible for the shareholder records?

Examples:

BNY Mellon

State Street Corporation

Computershare

If a TA exists → the system is not fully on-chain.

3️⃣ Who controls minting and burning?

Ask:

Which system or entity authorizes mint and burn operations?

Possible models:

Mint/Burn control	Architecture
Transfer agent system	Mirror
TA platform API	Digital TA
Smart contract logic	On-chain

This defines the token orchestration layer.

4️⃣ Where is compliance enforced?

Ask:

Where are KYC, eligibility, and transfer restrictions enforced?

Possible answers:

Compliance layer	Model
TA / admin system	Mirror
Compliance engine + smart contract	Hybrid
Smart contract whitelist	On-chain
5️⃣ Is the blockchain the legal record?

Ask explicitly:

Is the blockchain ledger considered the legal record of ownership?

Possible answers:

Answer	Meaning
No	Mirror
Partially	Hybrid
Yes	On-chain

In the U.S., regulators like the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission generally expect an official registry.

6️⃣ Is reconciliation required?

Ask:

Is reconciliation required between blockchain balances and the official registry?

If reconciliation exists:

OSS ledger
   ↔
Blockchain balances

then the architecture is Mirror OSS.

7️⃣ What role does the blockchain play?

Ask:

What is the role of the blockchain in the platform?

Possible roles:

Blockchain role	Architecture
Representation of shares	Mirror
Transfer layer	Hybrid
Official ledger	On-chain
8️⃣ What entity controls investor wallets?

Ask:

How are wallets linked to investor identities?

Possible answers:

Wallet model	Architecture impact
TA whitelist	Mirror
Compliance engine	Hybrid
Self custody allowed	On-chain
9️⃣ What happens during subscription?

Ask:

Which event happens first: registry update or token mint?

Two models exist:

Mirror model

TA update
   ↓
Token mint

On-chain model

Token mint
   ↓
Registry update

This tells you which system is authoritative.

🔟 What blockchain is used?

Ask:

Which blockchain network is used?

Common options include:

Ethereum

permissioned networks

private chains

The chain influences:

custody

compliance

settlement model

📊 The architecture decision tree

Once you answer the first three questions, the architecture becomes obvious.

Where is the shareholder registry?

         │
         ├── Transfer agent
         │       → Mirror OSS
         │
         ├── Digital TA platform
         │       → Hybrid architecture
         │
         └── Smart contract
                 → Fully on-chain
🧠 The three questions that matter most

If you only ask three things, ask these:

1️⃣ Where is the official shareholder register maintained?
2️⃣ Who controls mint/burn of tokens?
3️⃣ Is the blockchain the legal record of ownership?

These three answers determine 90% of the architecture.

✅ For your IT architecture work

If the answers indicate:

Transfer agent registry

mint controlled off-chain

blockchain not legal ledger

then you must design a Mirror OSS architecture.

That means your system must include:

token orchestration layer

reconciliation service

TA integration

compliance engine
